Indicator definitions & methodology

What each indicator measures and how it is computed. All figures are a compilation and computation of public market data (TWSE / TAIFEX). Not investment advice.

Smart-money positioning
The combined net position (spot + futures) of the three institutional groups — foreign investors, investment trusts, and dealers — ranked against its own 252-trading-day history and mapped to 1–10. It can be computed for foreign investors, domestic institutions (trusts + dealers), or all three combined.
Retail heat
The average of three 252-day percentile ranks — retail spot absorption, change in margin balance, and overall turnover — mapped to 0–10.
Market temperature
The average of smart-money positioning and retail heat — the headline "Total" reading — mapped to 0–10; foreign, domestic and retail can each also be read on their own. Shown as a daily gauge or a six-month trend.
GEX · Gamma exposure
The total gamma exposure that option market-makers must hedge to stay position-neutral, expressed as the NT$ amount adjusted per one-point index move (millions). Aggregated strike by strike as Black-Scholes gamma × open interest; implied volatility is solved from settlement prices.
Gamma center (pinning)
The strike band where gamma exposure is most concentrated on a given day.
Volume profile
Vertical volume is daily turnover over time; horizontal volume (volume profile) is the distribution of traded volume across price, whose thickest band is the value area.
TWVIX · volatility (HV21)
The annualised standard deviation of the index's daily returns over the last 21 trading days.

· The sign of dealers' gamma position is currently computed from a common assumption and remains to be verified against Taiwan data; its polarity is indicative only.

· GEX includes only options more than 2 days from expiry and near at-the-money; deep out-of-the-money and last-day contracts are excluded.

· In retail heat, the margin-balance change is a supplementary input — an increase may reflect either active buying or margin top-ups.

· TWVIX is a self-computed volatility proxy (HV21), not the official TAIFEX volatility index.

· GEX history begins in 2024, shorter than the index.

· All content is a compilation and computation of public market data; it contains no analysis or advice on individual securities and is not investment advice. See About.
