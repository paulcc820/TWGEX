Market temperature (0 cold ~ 10 hot)

The average of smart-money direction and retail heat: when institutions and retail are both leaning long and active, temperature is high (hot); when both are short and quiet, it is low (cold). It is a state reading of overall market sentiment and participation, not a trade signal. It can be computed using foreign, domestic, or all three institutional groups, and switched between gauge (latest close) and trend (last six months).

Retail heat

The average of three 252-day percentile ranks — retail spot absorption, change in margin balance, and overall turnover — mapped to 0 (cold) to 10 (hot).

Smart-money direction

The combined net position (spot + futures) of institutions ranked against its own 252-day history and mapped to 1 (most short) to 10 (most long). A structural-bias indicator, not a trade signal.
