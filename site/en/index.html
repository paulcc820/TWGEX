Gamma Exposure (GEX) · NT$M per index point

The total gamma exposure option market-makers hold, expressed as the NT$ amount adjusted per one-point index move (millions). When GEX is high, market-maker hedging tends to compress index volatility; when low, hedging may amplify it. The dotted marker on Panel A is the day's gamma center — the strike band where open interest is most concentrated. Smoothing can be set to Raw (showing the expiry-week gamma cycle) or 5/10/20-day (smoothed to a structural trend).

Volume profile (vertical + horizontal)

Vertical volume is daily turnover over time; horizontal volume (volume profile) is the distribution of traded volume across price, whose thickest band (the value area) is the most-traded price zone. Read alongside the gamma center, it shows where the market is anchored.

Smart-money positioning (foreign / domestic / total)

The combined net position (spot + futures) of institutions, ranked against its own 252-trading-day history and mapped to 1 (most short) to 10 (most long). Switchable between foreign, domestic (trusts + dealers), or all three combined; a divergence between foreign and domestic reflects differing institutional positioning. A structural-bias indicator, not a trade signal.
